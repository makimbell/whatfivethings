What five things...

...do you need to start playing guitar?

Guitar

This steel-string acoustic guitar is a great starting guitar for anyone. It doesn’t sacrifice quality for cost, and you’ll be happy to continue using it even after growing in skill.

Tuner

One essential piece of equipment for a guitar (at least until you can do it by ear) is a tuner. This one has a large color display that is easy to read and clips onto your guitar while you use it. It’s reliable, durable, and accurate, which is all a guitar tuner needs to be.

Stand

Were you just going to lean your brand new guitar against the wall? Try this sturdy guitar stand instead. It has plenty of advantages over leaning your guitar against the wall: it was made to hold guitars, it won’t scratch the wall, and it won’t drop your guitar. Make sure you check “other sellers” for a better price — it is often listed for $12 or less.

Book

There are plenty of online resources to go to when learning guitar. However, it’s often nice to have a single resource to teach you step-by-step as you learn something new. It’s also nice to have something in paper that you can flip through, highlight, and bookmark. This book is written for beginners and is very highly recommended.

Picks

Guitar picks are a dime a dozen, sometimes literally, and if you have a music store near you, you may as well buy them there. This variety pack is nice though, because it allows you to try picks of different thicknesses to find what best fits your playing style.
