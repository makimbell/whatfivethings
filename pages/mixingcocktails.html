What five things...

...do you need to start mixing cocktails?

Cocktail Shaker

If you’re serving James Bond, you better have one of these. This stainless steel cocktail shaker has a silicon seal, so you won’t be spraying liquid everywhere when you’re shaking up a martini. It looks great too, and it has a convenient spout for pouring.

Muddler

If you’re making a mojito, a genuine margarita, an old fashioned, or a number of other cocktails, you’ll need a muddler like this one to smash the juice and flavor out of your ingredients. Wood muddlers get gross over time, and nylon muddlers break in half, but this stainless steel muddler with a nylon head will stay looking sharp and new for years.

Strainer

Want to look like a professional bartender? Then use a strainer when you’re pouring drinks for your guests. Used to filter out bits of pulp, peel, ice, or anything else you don’t want in your final drink, a nice cocktail strainer like this one is an essential piece of a home bar.

Jigger

Many cocktail shakers, like the one listed on this page, have measurement lines in the removable cap over the pour spout. However, a jigger like this one is quicker and easier to use, and it’s an attractive and professional addition to a home bar.

Mixing Spoon

A mixing spoon is the perfect way to complete your professional bar setup. This stainless steel mixing spoon allows you to make fancy layered drinks, impress the ladies, and stir like a champion.
