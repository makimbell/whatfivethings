What five things...

...do you need to start geocaching?

Flashlight

Geocaches are often hidden in bushes, hollow logs, or other dark places. Having a bright, portable flashlight on you can really help you locate a cache when you know you’re close but you just can’t spot it.

Gloves

Geocaching often involves walking through wooded areas and digging around in places you might not want to put your delicate hands. These flexible work gloves will help protect your hands if you need to reach into any thorny bushes or porcupine nests.

Sunscreen

Going to be outside all day? Then this Family Size Banana Boat Sport Performance BROAD SPECTRUM SPF 50+ UVA/UVB protection sunscreen lotion with POWERSTAY TECHNOLOGY is exactly what you need. Swim. Splash. Sport.

Bug Spray

If you’re going into a wooded area, spray yourself with this first. It’ll protect you for 10 hours from mosquitoes, ticks, and everything else. If you don’t use this bug spray, then the bugs are going to use YOU.

Notebook

Lastly, make sure to bring a little notebook like this one. You’ll be able to record your found or unfound caches along with any notes you’d like to make to help other cachers out. You can also write a note and include it in the cache if it’s missing a log book or if you just want to leave a note for the next person to find.
