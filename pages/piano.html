What five things...

...do you need to start playing piano?

Digital Piano

If you need a great digital piano for the price, the Casio’s PX160 is one of the best. The weighted keys have a very genuine feel to them, and the sound through the built in speakers is crisp and accurate. This digital piano has an array of features and sounds, but they don’t disrupt its sleek and minimalist design.

Piano Stand

This solid stand is designed for the Casio PX150 linked on this page. You can get a stand for a lower price, but this one will turn your piano into an elegant piece of furniture that will look great in any room.

Bench

To go with a nice digital piano and stand, you’ll need a place to sit. This adjustable bench is solid and can hold well over 300 pounds… if that’s an issue for you. It’s solid black and will look great next to a black digital piano and stand.

Sustain Pedal

A sustain pedal is necessary for anything above beginner level playing. This one is of quality construction and works with any digital piano. The nonslip base will keep it exactly where you put it, and the action matches the feel of an acoustic piano pedal.

The Chord Wheel

Whether you’re taking lessons or teaching yourself to play, this book is a great reference tool. It has all the theory about keys and chord progressions that you’ll need to get started in understanding music.
