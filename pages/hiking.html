What five things...

...do you need to start hiking?

Equipment Kit

The easiest way to get started is by buying a kit like this one. It comes with almost everything you need to start brewing your own beer. The only other things you’ll need for Day 1 are a large stainless steel pot and the actual ingredients to your homebrew.

Stainless Steel Stockpot

The one piece of equipment missing from the kit listed here is a big stainless steel stockpot. You’ll need this to simmer your barley, malt extract, grain, hops, and whatever other flavors you’re infusing into your beer before fermenting.

Auto Siphon

An auto siphon makes the task of transferring liquids from one container to another, which is done twice in the brewing process, much easier. For “frustration avoided per dollar spent,” adding an auto siphon to your homebrew tool kit is a no-brainer.

Bottle Caps

You can either buy empty bottles or generate empty bottles from full bottles ifyouknowwhatimean, but either way you’ll need new bottle caps. Many ingredient kits come with bottle caps, but it’s always good to have extras around.

Bottle Filler

When it’s time to bottle your beer, you can either do it straight from the spigot on the bottling bucket, or you can attach the spigot to this bottle filler. Using a bottle filler allows you to fill the bottles from the bottom, which prevents your beer from splashing around and getting oxygen mixed in. It’s also less messy and less wasteful.

BONUS: Ingredients

What good does all this gear do without some ingredients? For a homebrew newbie, the easiest place to start out is by buying a kit of ingredients like this one. Northern Brewer kits get fantastic reviews, and this is one of their most popular ones. Once you have the equipment you need, buy this kit and get brewing!
